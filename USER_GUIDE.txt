1. INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

The LGU Document Tracking System is a web-based platform designed to track
and manage physical documents within Local Government Units (LGUs). The system
provides real-time tracking, QR code integration, status updates, and complete
audit trails for all document movements.

Key Features:
- Document creation and tracking
- QR code generation and scanning
- Real-time notifications
- Status management
- Priority flagging
- Archive system
- Complete document history
- Role-based access control

2. GETTING STARTED
================================================================================

2.1 LOGGING IN
--------------
1. Open your web browser and navigate to the system URL
2. Enter your email address and password
3. Click the "Login" button
4. If your account is pending verification, you will see a message
   indicating that an administrator needs to verify your account

2.2 REGISTERING A NEW ACCOUNT
------------------------------
1. Click on "Register" or "Sign Up" link
2. Fill in the registration form:
   - Full Name
   - Email Address
   - Password (must be at least 8 characters)
   - Confirm Password
   - Phone Number (optional)
   - Select your Role (LGU Staff, Department Head)
   - Select your Department
3. Click "Register"
4. Your account will be set to "Pending" status
5. Wait for an administrator to verify your account
6. Once verified, you will receive a notification and can log in

2.3 FIRST TIME LOGIN
--------------------
- After logging in, you will be redirected to your role-specific dashboard
- Take a moment to familiarize yourself with the navigation menu
- Check your notifications (bell icon in the top right)
- Update your profile information if needed

3. USER ROLES AND PERMISSIONS
================================================================================

3.1 ADMINISTRATOR
-----------------
Full system access - can see and do everything:
- View ALL documents in the system (not limited to department)
- Create, edit, and delete documents
- Verify and manage user accounts
- Set document priority levels
- Archive and restore documents
- Generate reports (PDF/DOCX)
- Scan QR codes (automatically receives documents)
- Complete documents with remarks
- Return documents with remarks
- Manage departments
- View comprehensive system statistics

3.2 MAYOR
---------
Similar to Administrator:
- View ALL documents
- Create and manage documents
- Set document priority
- Archive documents
- Generate reports (PDF/DOCX)
- Scan QR codes (automatically receives documents)
- Complete documents with remarks
- Return documents with remarks

Note: Mayor cannot manage users (only Administrator can).

3.3 DEPARTMENT HEAD
-------------------
Department-specific access (SAME as LGU Staff):
- View documents in their department
- View documents they created
- Generate Document QR
- Update document status
- Scan QR codes (automatically receives documents)
- Complete documents with remarks
- Return documents with remarks
- Archive documents (any document they can access)
- Generate reports (PDF/DOCX)
- Receive notifications

3.4 LGU STAFF
-------------
Staff-level access (SAME as Department Head):
- View documents in their department
- View documents they created
- Generate Document QR
- Update document status
- Scan QR codes (automatically receives documents)
- Complete documents with remarks
- Return documents with remarks
- Archive documents (any document they can access)
- Generate reports (PDF/DOCX)
- Receive notifications

IMPORTANT NOTES:
- Department Head and LGU Staff have IDENTICAL privileges. They can do the
  same things and see the same documents. The only difference is their job
  title, not their system permissions.

- Archive permissions are based on your ROLE, not who created the document.
  If you are Administrator, Mayor, Department Head, or LGU Staff, you can
  archive any document you can access, even if you didn't create it.

- Only Administrator can see ALL documents and manage users. All other
  roles see only documents in their department or documents they created.

5. DOCUMENT MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

5.1 CREATING A DOCUMENT
-----------------------
1. Navigate to "Documents" in the main menu
2. Click the "Create Document" button (green button)
3. Fill in the document form:
   - Title (required): Enter a clear, descriptive title
   - Description (optional): Add any relevant details
   - Document Type (required): Select from:
     * Certification
     * Disbursement Voucher
     * Leave Form
     * Obligation Request
     * Program of the Work
     * SEF
     * Service Record
     * Others (if selected, enter custom type)
   - Department (required): Select the target department
   - Priority (optional): Check if this is a priority document
4. Click "Create Document"
5. The system will:
   - Generate a unique document number (e.g., DOC-202410-0001)
   - Create a QR code automatically
   - Set status to "Forwarded"
   - Notify the target department
   - Log the creation in document history

5.2 VIEWING DOCUMENTS
---------------------
1. Go to "Documents" from the main menu
2. Use filters to find documents:
   - Search: Enter document number, title, or description
   - Status: Filter by Active, Received, Return, Completed, or All Status
   - Department: Filter by department (Administrators/Mayor only)
   - Priority: Show only priority documents
3. Click on a document title or "View" to see details
4. Document details page shows:
   - Document information
   - Current status
   - Current location (department)
   - Last location
   - QR code
   - Document history/timeline
   - Action buttons

5.3 UPDATING DOCUMENT STATUS
-----------------------------
1. Open the document you want to update
2. Scroll to the "Update Status" section
3. Select the new status from the dropdown:
   - Forwarded: Document is being sent to another department
   - Received: Document has been received
   - Under Review: Document is being reviewed
   - Pending: Waiting for action
   - Return: Document is being returned (requires remarks)
   - Completed: Document processing is finished
   - Approved: Document is approved (auto-archives)
4. If forwarding, select the target department
5. Add remarks (optional but recommended)
6. Click "Update Status"
7. The system will:
   - Update the document status
   - Log the change in history
   - Notify relevant users
   - Auto-archive if status is "Approved" or "Completed"

5.4 EDITING A DOCUMENT
----------------------
1. Open the document you want to edit
2. Click "Edit Document" button
3. Modify the fields:
   - Title
   - Description
   - Document Type
   - Department
   - Forward to Department (optional)
4. Click "Update Document"
5. If you selected "Forward to Department", the document will be
   forwarded to the new department automatically

5.5 SETTING PRIORITY
--------------------
(Administrators and Mayor only)
1. Open the document
2. Click "Set Priority" or "Remove Priority" button
3. Priority documents will:
   - Display with a priority badge
   - Show in priority filters
   - Send notifications to relevant users
   - Appear in priority sections on dashboards

5.6 ARCHIVING A DOCUMENT
------------------------
(Who can archive: Administrator, Mayor, Department Head, and LGU Staff)

1. Open the document you want to archive
2. Click "Archive Document" button
3. Confirm the action
4. The document will be:
   - Marked as "Archived"
   - Moved to the archive section
   - Removed from active documents list
   - Still searchable in archive

Note: You can archive documents based on your ROLE, not because you created
them. If you have one of the roles listed above, you can archive any document
you can access, even if someone else created it.

5.7 DELETING A DOCUMENT
-----------------------
(Administrators only)
1. Open the document
2. Click "Delete Document" button
3. Confirm the deletion
4. Warning: This action is permanent and cannot be undone
5. The document and its QR code will be permanently deleted

5.8 DOCUMENT STATUSES EXPLAINED
--------------------------------
- Forwarded: Document has been sent to a department
- Received: Document has been received by the department
- Under Review: Document is currently being reviewed
- Pending: Document is waiting for action
- Return: Document is being returned to previous department
- Completed: Document processing is finished (auto-archived)
- Approved: Document is approved (auto-archived)
- Rejected: Document was rejected
- Archived: Document has been manually archived

5.9 DOCUMENT FILTERING
----------------------
The Documents page supports multiple filters:
- Search: Search by document number, title, or description
- Status Filter:
  * All Status: Shows all non-archived documents
  * Active: Shows documents not yet completed
  * Received: Shows documents with "Received" status
  * Return: Shows documents with "Return" status
  * Completed: Shows completed documents
- Department Filter: Filter by department (Admin/Mayor only)
- Priority Filter: Show only priority documents

6. QR CODE SCANNING
================================================================================

6.1 ACCESSING THE SCANNER
--------------------------
1. Click "Scan" in the main navigation menu
2. The scanner page will open with an input field
3. The input field is optimized for hardware scanners (auto-focus)

6.2 SCANNING A DOCUMENT
-----------------------
Method 1: Hardware Scanner (Recommended)
1. Connect your USB or wireless 2D barcode scanner
2. Navigate to the Scan page
3. Click in the "Document Number" input field
4. Scan the QR code on the document
5. The scanner will automatically input the document number
6. The system will process the scan automatically

Method 2: Manual Entry
1. Go to the Scan page
2. Type or paste the document number
3. Press Enter or click "Scan"

6.3 WHAT HAPPENS WHEN YOU SCAN
------------------------------
When you scan a document (for ALL roles):
1. The system finds the document
2. The document is AUTOMATICALLY RECEIVED:
   - Status automatically changes to "Received"
   - Document location updates to your department
   - Tracking information is logged
3. Document details are shown immediately
4. You will see action buttons to:
   - Complete Document (with remarks)
   - Return Document (with remarks)
   - View Full Details
5. Relevant users are notified

Note: The document is automatically received when scanned. You don't need to
manually change the status to "Received" - it happens automatically.

6.4 QUICK ACTIONS AFTER SCANNING
--------------------------------
After scanning, the document is automatically received. Then you can:

1. COMPLETE DOCUMENT (all roles can do this):
   - Click "Complete Document" button
   - Add remarks (optional but recommended)
   - Document becomes "Completed" and is automatically archived
   - Notifications sent to creator and administrators

2. RETURN DOCUMENT (all roles can do this):
   - Click "Return Document" button
   - Enter remarks explaining why it's being returned (required)
   - Document returns to the previous department
   - Notifications sent to receiving department

3. VIEW FULL DETAILS:
   - Click "View Details" to see complete document information
   - Access all document management features
   - View complete document history

Note: All roles (Administrator, Mayor, Department Head, and LGU Staff) can
complete or return documents after scanning.

6.5 RETURNING A DOCUMENT
------------------------
(All roles can return documents)

1. Scan the document (it will be automatically received)
2. Click "Return Document" button
3. Enter remarks explaining why it's being returned (required)
4. Click "Return"
5. The document will:
   - Status changes to "Return"
   - Returns to the previous department that handled it
   - Logs the return with your remarks
   - Notifies the receiving department

6.6 COMPLETING A DOCUMENT
-------------------------
(All roles can complete documents)

1. Scan the document (it will be automatically received)
2. Click "Complete Document" button
3. Add remarks (optional but recommended)
4. Click "Complete"
5. The document will:
   - Status changes to "Completed"
   - Automatically archived
   - Notifications sent to creator and administrators
   - Logged in document history

6.7 PRINTING QR CODES
---------------------
1. Open the document
2. Click "Print QR Code" button
3. A print-friendly page will open
4. Use your browser's print function (Ctrl+P or Cmd+P)
5. The QR code can be printed and attached to the physical document

--------------------------

------------------------------
- Notifications automatically refresh every 30 seconds
- The unread count badge updates automatically
- No need to manually refresh the page

8. ARCHIVE SYSTEM
================================================================================

8.1 ACCESSING THE ARCHIVE
-------------------------
1. Click "Archive" in the main navigation menu
2. Only completed documents (status="Completed") are shown in archive
3. Archived documents are searchable and viewable

8.2 VIEWING ARCHIVED DOCUMENTS
------------------------------
1. Go to the Archive page
2. Use search to find specific documents
3. Use date filters to narrow results:
   - From Date: Start date
   - To Date: End date
4. Click on a document to view full details
5. Archived documents show complete history

8.3 RESTORING ARCHIVED DOCUMENTS
---------------------------------
1. Open the archived document
2. Click "Restore Document" button
3. Confirm the restoration
4. The document will:
   - Status changes to "Under Review"
   - Removed from archive
   - Added back to active documents
   - Logged in document history

8.4 PERMANENTLY DELETING ARCHIVED DOCUMENTS
-------------------------------------------
(Administrators only)
1. Open the archived document
2. Click "Delete Permanently" button
3. Confirm the deletion
4. Warning: This action cannot be undone
5. The document and all associated data will be permanently removed

8.5 ARCHIVE VS ACTIVE DOCUMENTS
-------------------------------
- Active Documents Page: Shows Forwarded, Received, Return, and Completed
  documents that are not manually archived
- Archive Page: Shows only Completed documents (status="Completed" with
  archived_at set)
- Manually archived documents (status="Archived") are excluded from both
  pages but can be restored

12. TROUBLESHOOTING
================================================================================

12.1 CANNOT LOG IN
------------------
- Verify your email and password are correct
- Check if your account is verified (contact administrator)
- Clear browser cache and cookies
- Try a different browser
- Contact system administrator

12.2 DOCUMENT NOT FOUND WHEN SCANNING
-------------------------------------
- Verify the document number is correct
- Check if document exists in the system
- Ensure you have permission to view the document
- Try searching for the document manually

12.3 NOTIFICATIONS NOT APPEARING
---------------------------------
- Check if notifications are enabled for your account
- Refresh the page
- Clear browser cache
- Check if you have the correct permissions
- Verify your account status is "Verified"

12.4 CANNOT UPDATE DOCUMENT STATUS
----------------------------------
- Verify you have permission to update documents
- Check if document is archived (archived documents cannot be updated)
- Ensure you selected a valid status
- Try refreshing the page

12.5 QR CODE NOT PRINTING
-------------------------
- Check printer connection
- Verify print settings in browser
- Try printing from a different browser
- Ensure pop-ups are not blocked

12.6 PAGE NOT LOADING
---------------------
- Check internet connection
- Clear browser cache
- Try a different browser
- Contact system administrator if problem persists

13. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q: How do I know if a document is priority?
A: Priority documents display a priority badge and appear in priority filters.

Q: Can I edit a document after creating it?
A: Yes, if you are the creator, an administrator, or mayor. Click "Edit
   Document" on the document page.

Q: What happens when I scan a QR code?
A: The document is AUTOMATICALLY received (status changes to "Received" and
   location updates to your department). Then you can immediately choose to
   complete the document or return it with remarks. No manual status update
   to "Received" is needed.

Q: Who can complete or return documents after scanning?
A: ALL roles (Administrator, Mayor, Department Head, and LGU Staff) can
   complete or return documents after scanning. The document is automatically
   received when scanned, then you can choose to complete or return it.

Q: How do I return a document?
A: Scan the document (it will be automatically received), click "Return
   Document", enter required remarks, and click "Return". The document will
   return to the previous department.

Q: Who can archive documents?
A: Users with roles Administrator, Mayor, Department Head, or LGU Staff can
   archive documents. Archive permission is based on your ROLE, not whether
   you created the document. If you have one of these roles, you can archive
   any document you can access.

Q: Can LGU Staff and Department Head do the same things?
A: Yes, LGU Staff and Department Head have IDENTICAL privileges. They can do
   the same actions and see the same documents. Only Administrators have
   additional privileges like viewing all documents and managing users.

Q: Can I generate reports for documents?
A: Yes, all roles can generate reports (PDF or DOCX format) for documents
   they have access to view. Simply open the document and click "Generate
   Report". This feature is available to Administrator, Mayor, Department
   Head, and LGU Staff.

Q: What's the difference between Archive and Completed?
A: Completed documents (status="Completed") are automatically archived and
   appear in the Archive page. Manually archived documents (status="Archived")
   are excluded from both pages until restored.

Q: Can I delete a document?
A: Only administrators can delete documents. Regular users can archive documents
   but not delete them.

Q: How do I know which documents are assigned to me?
A: Check your dashboard or filter documents by status. Documents forwarded to
   your department will appear in your department's document list.

Q: What should I do if I receive a document by mistake?
A: Use the "Return Document" feature to send it back to the previous
   department with remarks explaining the issue.

Q: How long are documents kept in the archive?
A: Documents remain in the archive indefinitely unless permanently deleted by
   an administrator.

Q: Can I change my role?
A: No, only administrators can change user roles. Contact your administrator if
   you need a role change.

Q: What document types are available?
A: Certification, Disbursement Voucher, Leave Form, Obligation Request,
   Program of the Work, SEF, Service Record, and Others (custom type).

Q: How do I know when a document status changes?
A: You will receive a notification when documents you created or documents in
   your department have status changes.

Q: Can I forward a document to multiple departments?
A: No, a document can only be in one department at a time. You can forward it
   to different departments sequentially.

Q: What happens if I archive a document by mistake?
A: You can restore archived documents by opening them and clicking "Restore
   Document".
